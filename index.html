Win Fast or Lose Slow: Balancing Speed and Accuracy in Latency-Sensitive Decisions of LLMs
Hao Kang, Qingru Zhang, Han Cai, Weiyuan Xu, Tushar Krishna, Yilun Du, Tsachy Weissman
Georgia Tech Nvidia Harvard Stanford

Overview

We propose a new benchmark for evaluating the speed-accuracy tradeoffs of LLM agents in latency-sensitive tasks. Our benchmark consists of two tasks: StreetFighter, a competitive game, and HFTBench, a high-frequency trading task. We evaluate the performance of LLM agents on these tasks using three different serving methods: API, local, and remote. We find that the speed-accuracy tradeoffs of LLM agents are significantly different depending on the serving method. Our benchmark provides a new perspective on the speed-accuracy tradeoffs of LLM agents and can be used to guide the development of more efficient and accurate LLM agents.

Key Features

  • Diverse Benchmarks: StreetFighter and HFT trading tasks.
  • Flexible Agent Deployment: API/local/remote LLM serving support.
  • System-Aware Evaluation: Analyze latency-performance tradeoffs.
